<analysis>**original_problem_statement:**
The user wants to build a platform to launch and monitor fully autonomous agents, called Animas, in isolated, sandboxed environments. The core of the platform is a prediction-to-execution engine called Ultimus (a fork of MiroFish/OASIS) which simulates complex scenarios and generates mission plans (genesis prompts) for the Animas.

The platform should support a Bring Your Own Infrastructure (BYOI) model, allowing Animas to be deployed on any cloud provider or the user's own network. Animas must be truly autonomous: they should have their own wallets, pay for their own operational costs (compute, tools), and be able to spawn child Animas to scale their operations, all guided by their genesis prompt.

**PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS:**
1.  **Platform Control Plane:** A central dashboard to create the first founder Anima, monitor all Animas, and manage BYOI and inference provider configurations.
2.  **Ultimus Simulation Engine:** A forked and rebranded version of MiroFish/OASIS. It takes a high-level goal from the user, runs a multi-agent simulation, and outputs a detailed execution plan, including a cost model and specific genesis prompts for each required Anima.
3.  **Anima Runtime (OpenClaw Fork):** The core of each Anima. A rebranded fork of OpenClaw, giving the Anima full capabilities (browser, shell, memory, self-modification, skills). It will be extended with a native wallet (using x402 for payments) and state-reporting capabilities.
4.  **Directed Autonomy:** Animas operate autonomously but are directed by their genesis prompt. The user or the Ultimus simulation defines the mission constraints, but the Anima has the flexibility to decide *how* to achieve its goals within those constraints.
5.  **Self-Propagating Economy:** Animas are seeded with initial funding. They must generate revenue to cover their own costs and fund the creation of child Animas, allowing the network to grow and execute the simulation's plan autonomously.
